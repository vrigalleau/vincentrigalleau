SO296-2 Mapuche / Expedition in the Patagonian fjords

February 2023 • Patagonia

Another Expedition on the Sonne! This time we were exploring the patagionian fjord, notably the Golfo Almirante Montt. We also focused on the fjord system Canal Concepción/Canal Wide/Seno. We addressed the oceanographic situation by studying the interaction between offshore postglacial currents and water mass dynamics and the fjords glaciation and sedimentation history in detail.


 

SO290 PaleoTaNZ / Expedition onboard Sonne

April to May 2022 • Tasman Sea

This cruise in the southeastern Tasman Sea had for main objectives the reconstruction of paleoceanographic and climatic changes in the southeastern Tasman Sea over the last glacial-interglacial cycles, and the reconstruction of the glacial history of the Southern Alps of New Zealand over the last glacial-interglacial cycles. Additionally, the high amounts of terrigenous material supplied to the Tasman Sea from the glaciated Southern Alps of New Zealand as well as Australia make this an ideal region to reconstruct glacier changes and dust variations over the recent geologic past.


At the end of the expedition, in Nouméa, I was interviewed for France TV coverage. They were happy to have a french scientist on this german expedition! mission scientifique de haut niveau pour le navire de recherche allemand Sonne

 

PS128 EASI-1 /Expedition onboard the Polarstern

January to February 2022 • Antarctica

On the Polarstern, the icebreaker from AWI, I went on a 2 months expedition to directly record the "East Antarctic Ice Sheet Instabilities". I was there as a sedimentologist and I spent lot of time on the deck moving the heavy coring devices. Notably, one of the sediment core we recovered on the Bungenstock plateau was studied by my colleague and friend Taina for his PhD project. You can find the study here.

Fieldtrip in New Zealand

November to December 2019 • Southland,

For the "MAOREE" project of Sam Toucanne, I was driving around, sampling the Southland, Otago and Canterburry rivers, one of the most beautiful places (after Vendée of course)!
The goal was to sample the geochemical "signature" of the sediment transported by the river, more precisely isotopic signature of neodymium (Nd). Some impressions, my car that broke down, the time sampling next to the beautiful rivers and working in a sheep farm!
